# Crypto — January 15, 2025

## Bitcoin ETF Inflows Drive Broad Crypto Rally

Spot Bitcoin exchange-traded funds attracted more than **$500 million in net inflows** on January 15 — the largest single-day figure since the products launched. The surge of institutional capital pushed Bitcoin briefly above $50,000 during the Asian session before the price settled back to $49,400 as early buyers took profits.

### ETF Demand Extends to Ethereum

The bullish mood was not confined to Bitcoin. Ethereum spot ETFs mirrored the activity, recording their **second-highest inflow day** since approval. Analysts noted that the parallel demand signals a structural shift toward crypto as a mainstream institutional asset class rather than a speculative outlier.

### What This Means

The size and breadth of Wednesday's inflows suggest that the early months of spot ETF trading are attracting capital from pension funds, family offices, and wealth management platforms that previously had no compliant on-ramp to crypto exposure. If the pace continues, it would represent a multi-billion dollar monthly allocation from traditional finance.

### Outlook

Market participants will watch whether inflows sustain above $300 million per day over the next week. A sustained run at that level would put cumulative ETF holdings on track to rival the largest gold ETFs within twelve months — a milestone that would validate the bull case for Bitcoin as a reserve asset.
