# Finance — January 15, 2025

## Fed Minutes Kill Rate-Cut Hopes, Pushing Equities Lower

Minutes from the December Federal Open Market Committee meeting, released on January 15, confirmed that officials saw **no urgency to cut interest rates** in the near term. The hawkish signal sent US equity markets sharply lower, with the S&P 500 closing down 1.2%.

### What the Minutes Said

FOMC participants broadly agreed that the federal funds rate should remain at its current level until incoming data provided greater confidence that inflation was on a sustained path back to the 2% target. Several members flagged upside risks to inflation from labour market resilience and strong consumer spending.

### Market Reaction

The immediate response was unambiguous. The S&P 500 fell 1.2%, with rate-sensitive sectors bearing the brunt:

- **Real estate** (XLRE): −2.4%
- **Utilities** (XLU): −1.8%
- **Financials** (XLF): −0.9%

Bond markets moved in parallel — the 10-year Treasury yield climbed 8 basis points to 4.18%, its highest level in three weeks.

### Dollar Strengthens

The US dollar index (DXY) rose to its highest point in two months as the rate repricing pushed EUR/USD briefly below 1.09. A stronger dollar typically acts as a headwind for US multinationals and emerging market economies with dollar-denominated debt.

### Outlook

Markets will now focus on January CPI data due in the coming weeks. A softer-than-expected print could revive rate-cut expectations and provide relief to equities. A hotter print would likely extend the recent sell-off.
